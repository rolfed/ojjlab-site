A Career Built on the Mats

Adam Fox has spent 24 years studying, competing, and coaching Brazilian Jiu Jitsu. His path has taken him from the competition circuit to the national team coaching level, working across continents to develop athletes in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan.

As head coach of the Kazakhstan National Jiu Jitsu Team and at Golden Eagle Jiu Jitsu and Wrestling in Kyrgyzstan, Adam built programs from the ground up — developing athletes who competed on the world stage and won.

The fighters he's coached have gone on to win IBJJF World Championships and compete professionally in the UFC. Those results aren't accidents — they're the output of a deliberate, structured approach to developing grapplers at every level.

"The mats don't care about your excuses. They only care about your consistency."

What Adam Believes About Training

Adam's coaching goes beyond technique. He builds athletes as complete people — developing physical capacity, mental discipline, and the kind of character that holds up under pressure. His students don't just improve on the mat; they become more capable in every part of their lives.

He believes the work you put into Jiu Jitsu compounds. A year of consistent training produces more than physical skill — it produces a different person. That belief shapes every class he teaches and every athlete he develops.

At OJJ Lab, Adam runs the curriculum, works the floor, and coaches students from their first day to competition camp. He is the standard the whole gym holds itself to.
