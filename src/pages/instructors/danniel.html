Two Decades, Two Continents

Danniel's Jiu Jitsu story started in 2002. Since then he's trained, competed, and taught across continents — from São Paulo, where the roots of BJJ run deep, to Oregon, where he now coaches at OJJ Lab.

He trains under Adam Fox and Erick Hemphil, continuing to develop his own game while building the next generation of students. That dual role — lifelong student and active teacher — shapes how he shows up on the mat every day.

Eight years of coaching have given Danniel a clear sense of what students actually need: not just technique, but the confidence to fail, try again, and grow. That belief drives every class he teaches.

"Jiu Jitsu is for everyone. It doesn't matter where you start — only that you show up."

How Danniel Teaches

Danniel's approach is grounded in one conviction: the mat should be accessible. Not easy — accessible. There's a difference. He sets a high standard for focus and effort, but he builds the environment that makes it possible for anyone to meet it.

His teaching style is direct and patient. He breaks technique down to its core, corrects errors without embarrassing students, and gives the kind of individualized feedback that turns confusion into clarity. Students who work with Danniel progress because they understand what they're doing — not just what they're imitating.

Whether you're a complete beginner who's never been on a mat, or an experienced practitioner working on a specific problem, Danniel meets you where you are. That's what makes him effective.
