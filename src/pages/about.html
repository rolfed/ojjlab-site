Who We Are

OJJ Lab was built on one idea: good technique, taught well, changes people.

We're not a fitness class or a hobbyist club. We're a serious training environment that happens to be welcoming to beginners. You don't need experience to start — just a willingness to show up and learn.

Our gym is in Hillsboro, Oregon and serves students from across the Portland metro area.

What We Teach

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is a ground-based martial art that uses leverage and technique over size and strength. It's practical, deeply technical, and one of the most effective self-defense systems in the world.

At OJJ Lab, we cover all core areas of the game — from standing to the ground.

  • ✓
    Fundamental positions & movements The building blocks every student needs before anything else.
  • ✓
    Takedowns & wrestling How to close distance, control the clinch, and get the fight to the ground.
  • ✓
    Guard passing & submissions The core technical elements of BJJ — both offense and defense.
  • ✓
    Live training (sparring) Controlled, supervised practice against resisting partners — the proof of the work.

Our Approach

We follow a structured curriculum so your progress is never random. There's a clear path — and every class moves you forward along it.

New students aren't thrown in the deep end. Classes are designed so all levels train together productively, with coaches giving individual feedback throughout.

  • ✓
    Structured curriculum A clear path from white belt to black belt — no guesswork about what to learn next.
  • ✓
    Individual coaching Coaches correct technique personally, not just at the front of the room.
  • ✓
    All levels train together Beginners learn from experienced partners. Advanced students sharpen their teaching.
  • ✓
    No ego culture Everyone here is to help each other improve. That standard is enforced.

What to Expect Your First Class

Walking into a BJJ gym for the first time can feel intimidating. We've heard that from almost every member we have now.

  1. 1
    Arrive 10 minutes early

    Introduce yourself at the front desk. We'll walk you around and answer questions before class starts.

  2. 2
    Join the warm-up

    All classes start with movement drills. Follow along at your own pace — no one is watching to judge.

  3. 3
    Learn the day's technique

    The coach demonstrates and breaks down the technique. Then you practice with a partner at a controlled pace.

  4. 4
    No pressure to spar on day one

    Live training at the end of class is optional for new students. We'll let you know when you're ready.

Your first class is free. No contract, no commitment.
